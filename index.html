The Way to New Life

What is the Gospel?


John 3:16
"For God so loved the world that he gave his one and only Son, that whoever believes in him shall not perish but have eternal life."

We all make mistakes, which is called sin. Sin seperates us from God, because God is perfect. The penalty of sin is death, but God loves us so much that he wouldn't let us be seperated from him. Jesus Christ, who is God, came down in human flesh, lived a perfect life free of sin, and willingly died on a cross. Jesus took all of our sins, paying the penalty for us through his own blood. However, he didn't stay dead. 3 days later Jesus rose from the grave, conquering sin and death for us. By having faith in Jesus with all your heart and declaring with your mouth that Jesus is Lord we can now be with God and have eternal life with him in heaven.


Where the greatest love story began...

The Birth of Christ

6-4 B.C

2,000+ years ago in the little town of Nazareth in Galilee, there was a virgin woman named Mary. Mary was a descendent of David, pledged to be married to a man named Joseph. One night, an angel of the Lord came with a message to her telling her she has found favor with God. The angel told her she would be the mother of the one who would save the people from their sins, the Messiah, the Son of God. Through the Holy Spirit, the power of God overshadowing her, Mary became pregnant. Several months later, Jesus was born in a town called Bethlehemm in Judea. Since the town had no rooms to keep Mary and Joseph in, Jesus was born is a stable on a manger. At this time Mary's relative Elizabeth was 6 months pregnant in her old age with the man who was to go before Jesus and prepare the way for Jesus. Prophesies from hundreds of years ago in the Old Testament were now being fulfilled in Jesus' life.

Jesus is Baptized

28-29 A.D

Once Jesus was 30 years old, he went to his cousin John who was to go before him. John was telling the people to Repent and turn to God, telling them the good news of the coming Messiah. Jesus went to him while he was baptizing people in the Jordan River and John told everybody that this was the Son of Man who was to come into the world and save the people from their sins. Jesus was then baptized by him, and when he came up out of the water, the Spirit of God landed on him like a dove and a voice from heaven could be heard by the people saying, "This is my Son, whom I love; with him I am well pleased."

Jesus is tempted by Satan

28-29 A.D

After Jesus was baptized he was lead by the Holy Spirit into the wilderness to be tempted. He fasted for 40 days and nights, and Satan came to him saying, “If you are the Son of God, tell these stones to become bread.” Jesus answered, “It is written: ‘Man shall not live on bread alone, but on every word that comes from the mouth of God.’” Then the devil took him to the holy city and had him stand on the highest point of the temple. “If you are the Son of God,” he said, “throw yourself down. For it is written: “‘He will command his angels concerning you, and they will lift you up in their hands, so that you will not strike your foot against a stone.’” Jesus answered him, “It is also written: ‘Do not put the Lord your God to the test.’” Again, the devil took him to a very high mountain and showed him all the kingdoms of the world and their splendor. “All this I will give you,” he said, “if you will bow down and worship me.” Jesus said to him, “Away from me, Satan! For it is written: ‘Worship the Lord your God, and serve him only.’” Then the devil left him, and angels came and attended him.

Jesus calls the 12 Disciples

28-29 AD

After Jesus was tempted he began his ministry and called his 12 disciples, (Simon) Peter, Andrew, Matthew, John, Judas Iscariot, James (son of Zebedee), Philip, Nathaneal, Thomas, Simon the Zealot, Thaddeus and James (son of Alphaeus). All ordinary men, sinners, some hated by many, but still have a purpose in Jesus.

Jesus Performs Miracles

28-30 AD

Once Jesus begins his ministry he begins performing many miracles throughout his time like turning water into wine, healing the sick, restoring sight to the blind, giving a voice to the mute, hearing to the deaf, bringing the dead back to life, and multiplying food for multitudes. No one had ever seen this before, many people believed he was the Messiah, but the Jewish Pharisees at the time thought he was blasphemous and they were jealous of him because they wanted to be the honored ones. Jesus taught what was different from the corrupt teachings they taught.

Jesus Teaches the Truth

28-33 AD

Jesus in his ministry begins teaching the people many things, through parables and miracles. The Sermon on the mount is one example. He taught the truth which was contrary to the false teaching of what the Pharisees and teachers of the law were teaching, telling people that to love your neighbor as yourself (even your enemies), serve others rather than yourself, and to love God with all your heart, mind, soul and strength fulfilled the whole law. Jesus also taught that he had authority to forgive sins, and that the people could not be saved by religious sacrafices or works, but only by faith could they be cleansed of their sins. Jesus taught that he was the way, the truth and the life, and nobody can come to the father except through him. He taught grace, love and mercy while still teaching the truth about what would happen to the people who rejected him.

Jesus is Questioned and Hated

28-33 AD

The Pharisees and Sadducees would question Jesus, make plots to harm him and his disciples, and try to stone him for his teachings even though Jesus lived a perfect life doing no wrong, never sinning. He didn't obey the human rules that the Pharisees wanted him too. He was a friend of sinners, he healed even on the sabbath, and he called the Pharisees the brood of vipers, woeing them for their ways, because they appear to be close to God by their actions, but in their hearts, Jesus saw that they were far from him. They hardened their hearts to Jesus. Jesus knew that they were blind, couldn't understand his ways, and wanted him killed, not for God, but so they could keep their power.

Jesus is Anointed at Bethany

30-33 AD

During Holy Week, Jesus was invited to one of his closest friend Lazarus' house for a dinner to honor him for raising him from the dead in order to glorify God so that people may believe that Jesus is the Son of God. During this dinner Mary poured a pint of pure nard, expensive perfume, on Jesus' feet and wiped his feet with her hair anointing him before his burial that would soon come.

The Last Supper

30-33 AD

Jesus and his diciples were in the upper room of a certain mans house eating the Passover meal. Jesus would go on to tell the disciples that he would be flogged, mocked and killed, but told them to not be afraid because he would later rise 3 days later. He tells the disciples through the symbols of bread and wine that the bread is broken as his body is for us, and the wine is his blood spilled out for us to wash us clean from our sins.

Jesus in Gethsemane

30-33 AD

Jesus, Peter, James and John went to the garden of Gethsemane to pray, the night of which he would be betrayed and handed over to the Jewish Leaders. His soul was sorrowful, praying, asking the Father if there was another way, but he said not my will but yours be done. Jesus prayed 3 times and after Judas came with a crowd of armed romans to betray him because Jesus didn't meet his expectations of who he wanted him to be and do, he didn't understand that Jesus' kingdom was not of that world but of heaven, he thought he was going to overthrow Rome who held Israel in its clutches. Judas tells the romans who Jesus is and Jesus willingly goes with them.

Jesus sentenced to Crucifixion

30-33 AD

Jesus gets persecuted by the Sanhedrin (the Jewish Council), proclaiming also that he is God. The Sanhedrin found him guilty of blasphemy and brought him before Pilate (the Roman Govenor) to be sentenced to death. The custom at the festival of the Passover was to let one of the prisoners go. They had Jesus Barabbas and Jesus the Messiah, and the Pharisees sturred up the crowd to let Barabbas go and crucify Jesus the Messiah. Pilate had to give in to the crowd, though wanting nothing to do with killing Jesus, and Jesus was sentenced to crucifixion.

Jesus Crucified

30-33 AD

Jesus was flogged with a cat of nine tails, whips, he was mocked. Jesus wore a crown of thorns piercing through his skull, He carried a 300 pound cross on his back through the streets of Jerusalem, going up to Golgotha (the place of the skull), outside the city of Jerusalem. This was the same place where Abraham was going to kill his son Isaac as a sacrafice to God in the Old Testament. When Jesus was hanging on the cross, he said to the people crucifying him, "father forgive them for they know not what they do." There were 2 thiefs being crucified next to Jesus, and one thief mocked Jesus along with the crowd telling Jesus to save himself if he was God. The other thief on the cross rebuked him, he said, "Our punishment is just, but this man is innocent, Jesus remember me when you enter into your kingdom." Jesus then told him, "Truly I tell you, today you will be with me in paradise." He cried out to God saying, "Eli Eli lema sabachthani," (my God my God why have you forsaken me) because he had taken on all the sins of the world. With his last breath he said, "it is finished," and so he gave up his spirit to God. The temple curtain to the tabernacle was torn in 2 from top to bottom, symbolizing that God and humans now had direct access to each other through Christ Jesus. Darkness fell and the earth rumbled. Even the Roman centurion exclaimed, "Surely this was the Son of God!"

The Empty Tomb

33 AD

Jesus was taken down from the cross by another of his disciples named Joseph of Arimathea, and was buried in an empty tomb that hadn't been used before. A giant stone was placed in front of the tomb. After the Sabbath, at dawn, on the first day of the week, Mary Magdalene and the Mary (Jesus' mother) went to the tomb, and found an angel of the Lord who told them, "do not be afraid, I know you are seeking Jesus, but he isn't here, he is risen, go tell the disciples, he is going ahead of you into Galilee." They ran to tell the disciples and saw Jesus on the way, and they worshipped him clasping his feet. Jesus appeared to his disciples many more times in the same flesh and appeared to over 500 different eye witnesses. Then after 40 days, his disciples watched him be taken up into heaven to sit at the right hand of God. Now anyone who believes in the name of the Son of God with all their heart and declares him as Lord, they will be saved. By his blood we are healed from our sin which seperated us from God, but now we are free by faith in his sacrafice for us and his unfailing forgiveness. Jesus loves you.
